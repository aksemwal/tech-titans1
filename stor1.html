Chapter 2

David’s pulse pounded as the train lurched violently, the flickering lights revealing something unsettling. The passengers—gone. The long-coated man, the woman across from him—vanished without a trace. The windows no longer showed the world outside, only an abyss of darkness stretching endlessly beyond the glass.

A whisper slithered through the empty car. Too late… too late… too late…

His fingers clenched around the crumpled ticket in his pocket. The silence pressed against him, thick and unnatural. He forced himself forward, pushing through the rattling door into the next car—only to stop dead in his tracks.

David swallowed hard. “What happens if I don’t get off?”

The conductor’s face darkened. “Then you stay.”



David hesitated. “Where is everyone?”

The conductor’s gaze flickered to the ticket in David’s hand. “You’re still holding it. That’s good.”

David swallowed hard. “What happens if I don’t get off?”

The conductor’s face darkened. “Then you stay.”
